# Positioning Statement — Strategic Intelligence Platform

**Framework**: April Dunford's "Obviously Awesome" 5-component positioning framework (2nd edition, Feb 2026)
**Date**: July 5, 2026
**Status**: Active (pilot phase)

---

## Positioning Statement

**For** enterprise strategy, geopolitical risk, and public-sector foresight teams **who** struggle to turn complex uncertainties into defensible, reviewable decision options, **Strategic Intelligence Platform** is a **governed strategic-intelligence pilot tool** that **connects evidence-gathering, game-theory analysis, human review, and forecast governance into one workflow** — **unlike** Recorded Future (threat-intel only, no game theory or forecast governance), Palantir AIP (enterprise-only, months of setup, no forecasting), and Metaculus/Good Judgment (forecasting only, no evidence-gathering or human-review workflow).

## 5-Component Breakdown

### 1. Competitive Alternatives
- **Recorded Future**: Threat intelligence platform ($50K-$150K+/year). Strong at data ingestion but no game theory, no forecast governance, no human-review workflow.
- **Palantir AIP**: Operational AI platform (custom enterprise pricing, $100K+/year). Strong at data integration but months of setup, no forecasting, no pilot path for small teams.
- **Metaculus / Good Judgment**: Forecasting communities and professional services. Strong at prediction accuracy but no evidence-gathering workflow, no game theory, no human-review state.

### 2. Unique Attributes
- Game theory engine (Nash equilibrium, Bayesian updating, quantum strategies) — no competitor has this
- Human-review workflow with reviewer accept/reject/send-back states
- Forecast governance with Brier scoring, pre-resolution capture, and publish-readiness gating
- Evidence-gated analysis with real citations — no black-box predictions
- Pilot-ready at $0-$199 (vs $50K-$150K+ for competitors)

### 3. Differentiated Value
- **One workflow from question to decision**: evidence → analysis → review → forecast — no competitor combines all four
- **Defensible outputs**: every recommendation has source citations, actor reasoning, and confidence intervals
- **Governed forecasts**: pre-resolution capture, leakage review, and calibration-aware scoring
- **Lightweight pilot path**: self-serve free tier + guided pilot, not months of enterprise procurement

### 4. Target Market Characteristics
- **Primary**: Corporate strategy teams, geopolitical risk analysts, public-sector foresight units, treasury/finance risk teams
- **Secondary**: Forecasting researchers, executive education programs, analysts at think tanks
- **Tertiary**: Educators, learners, and individual users interested in game theory and strategic reasoning
- **Champion**: The person inside the organization who needs defensible, source-backed analysis — not a black-box AI answer

### 5. Market Category
- **Category**: Strategic Intelligence Platform (not "game theory tool" or "AI prediction engine")
- **Frame**: "What if your strategic intelligence workflow had evidence-gathering, game-theory reasoning, human review, and forecast governance in one place — instead of spread across 4 disconnected tools?"

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Level 1: Core Message
"Governed strategic-intelligence pilot with evidence-backed decision support — not black-box AI predictions."

### Level 2: Benefit Pillars (3)
1. **Evidence-Backed Analysis**: Real citations from verified sources with actor reasoning and countermove maps
2. **Human Review Workflow**: Route contested or high-stakes output to reviewers before action
3. **Governed Forecasting**: Brier-scored forecasts with calibration-aware decision support and publish-readiness gating

### Level 3: Features
- Nash equilibrium computation with confidence intervals
- 26 strategic patterns (Prisoner's Dilemma to Quantum Strategies)
- GDELT global events monitoring (250M+ events)
- World Bank historical data (50+ years)
- Brier scoring and reliability calibration
- Pre-resolution forecast capture
- Reviewer accept/reject/send-back states
- Audience-tailored views (student, researcher, teacher, executive)

## What We Don't Claim (Claim Boundaries)
- No world-class prediction accuracy (no resolved-outcome calibration ledger)
- No enterprise-ready security (0/8 procurement documents ready)
- No buyer-validated willingness to pay (0 completed calls)
- No hosted/live proof (no deployed evidence)
- No Palantir-equivalence or forecasting-parity

## Dual Positioning Note

The platform serves both enterprise and consumer users. Enterprise pilot positioning leads. Consumer features (Personal Life Coach, Bias Detection, Conflict Mediator) are secondary and positioned as "Additional Modules" — not the headline value proposition.

**Rationale**: The GTM campaign kit targets enterprise buyers with 10 named accounts. Consumer features are valuable but don't drive revenue in the pilot phase. Leading with consumer positioning would confuse enterprise buyers who see "everyday people" language and dismiss the platform as a consumer app.
