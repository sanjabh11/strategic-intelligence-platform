# Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) — B2B Cold Email Outreach

**Date**: July 5, 2026
**Organization**: Ignite IT Serve
**Contact**: contact@igniteitserve.com
**Scope**: Founder-led B2B cold email outreach for Strategic Intelligence Platform pilot program

---

## 1. Purpose of Processing

**Purpose**: To contact enterprise and public-sector decision teams about a prelaunch pilot program for a strategic-intelligence platform.

**Why**: The platform is in a guided pilot phase. We need to identify teams who would benefit from evidence-backed strategic analysis, game theory modeling, and governed forecasting workflows. Founder-led outreach is the most appropriate channel for a prelaunch product.

## 2. Necessity Test

**Is processing necessary for the stated purpose?** Yes. Cold email is the primary channel for reaching enterprise decision-makers who are not already in our network. Alternative channels (LinkedIn, events, content marketing) are supplementary but cannot replace direct outreach for a prelaunch product with zero existing audience.

**Could the purpose be achieved without processing personal data?** No. We need to contact specific individuals (by name and role) at target organizations. Generic outreach to info@ addresses has negligible response rates for enterprise B2B.

## 3. Proportionality Test

**Data minimization**: We collect only:
- Business email address (from public company websites or professional directories)
- Recipient first name (for personalization)
- Job title / role (to verify relevance)
- Company name (to verify fit)

**Data sources**: Public business contact information from:
- Company websites (public team/about pages)
- Professional directories (Crunchbase, industry associations)
- LinkedIn (public profiles, manually collected — no automation)

**We do NOT collect**:
- Personal email addresses
- Phone numbers (unless publicly listed on company website)
- Home addresses
- Personal social media accounts
- Sensitive personal data (health, political opinions, etc.)

## 4. Balance Test (Impact on Data Subject)

**Could the recipient reasonably expect this contact?** Yes. The recipients are business professionals in roles related to strategic intelligence, risk analysis, forecasting, and public-sector foresight. The email content is directly relevant to their professional role.

**Is the content relevant to the recipient's role?** Yes. Each email template is tailored to a specific segment (corporate strategy, treasury risk, public-sector foresight, forecasting evaluation, executive education). We do not contact recipients whose roles are unrelated to the product.

**Is the frequency reasonable?** Yes. Maximum 2 follow-up emails per recipient over 4 weeks. No automated sequences. All outreach is manual and founder-led.

**Can the recipient easily opt out?** Yes. Every email includes:
- A clear unsubscribe instruction ("reply with 'unsubscribe' or email contact@igniteitserve.com")
- Sender identification (Ignite IT Serve)
- Contact email (contact@igniteitserve.com)

**Opt-out processing**: Unsubscribe requests are processed within 24 hours (faster than the 10 business days required by CAN-SPAM/CASL and the 24-48 hours expected by GDPR).

## 5. Data Retention

- Active prospect data: Retained for the duration of the outreach campaign (up to 90 days).
- Unsubscribed prospects: Added to suppression list within 24 hours. Email address retained on suppression list indefinitely to prevent re-contact.
- Completed/declined prospects: Retained for 2 years for audit purposes, then deleted.

## 6. Documentation

**Record keeping**: For each contact, we document:
- Data source (where the email was obtained)
- Date of first contact
- Segment/relevance rationale
- Response status (no response, replied, unsubscribed, requested demo)
- Opt-out date (if applicable)

**Records are stored**: In a private spreadsheet accessible only to the founder. Not shared with third parties.

## 7. Conclusion

The legitimate interest basis is appropriate for this B2B cold email outreach because:
1. The purpose is legitimate business development for a pilot program
2. The processing is necessary and proportionate
3. The data is minimized to business contact information from public sources
4. The content is relevant to the recipient's professional role
5. The recipient can easily opt out
6. The frequency is low and manual
